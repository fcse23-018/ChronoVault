The Eternal City

Ancient Rome, from 753 BCE to 476 CE, shaped Western civilization with its legions, aqueducts, and legal systems. Emperors like Augustus and structures like the Colosseum defined its legacy.

Colosseum, iconic arena of Roman spectacles.

Roman Forum, heart of political life.

Roman aqueduct, engineering marvel for water.

Gladius sword, weapon of Roman legions.

Roman mosaic, intricate villa decoration.

Toga, garment of Roman citizenship.

Key Figures

  • Augustus: Established the Roman Empire.
  • Julius Caesar: Expanded Roman territories.
  • Marcus Aurelius: Ruled during Pax Romana.
  • Cleopatra: Influenced Roman politics.

Meet Julius Caesar

Julius Caesar (100–44 BCE), a brilliant general and statesman, transformed Rome through conquests and reforms, paving the way for the Roman Empire.
